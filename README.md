## Who I Am
 
I manage judicial operations at Santa Barbara Superior Court. Ten years of studying how systems serve people, spotting where they fall short of their potential, and improving them from the inside.
 
I started building AI tools in July 2025 and I've shipped in public ever since: seventeen hackathons, five wins, three products live on their own domains. I use AI as a thinking partner, direct and validate what it produces, and ship things that hold up. Growth is measurable. Context shouldn't evaporate. Invisible problems deserve visible solutions.
 
Off the clock I speak to court leaders about AI adoption (NACM 2026, Los Angeles Superior Court). I'm also a Supernote devotee, a Lakers lifer, and firmly convinced that earl grey hot is the correct choice.

## 🧠 How I Build
 
I don't write code from scratch. I provide creative direction and validation: AI tools generate, I compare outputs, pick what works, and stitch them into something that holds up. That's a deliberate workflow, not a limitation.
 
**Occam's Razor** · Solve one problem completely before adding the next. The simplest solution that ships beats the ambitious one that crashes.
 
**Single Responsibility** · Every component does one thing well. No Swiss Army knives.
 
**Foundation First** · Live integrations over mocked features. Production architecture over quick hacks. This is always a v1, not a v-final.
 
**Operational Thinking** · Ten years in judicial systems means I know implementations have consequences. That instinct doesn't turn off.
